★☆☆   melancholy
 / ˈmelənkəli /◙ noun
1. [mass noun] a deep, pensive, and long-lasting sadness  • 忧思，愁思
  another term for melancholia (as a mental condition)  • 同 melancholia [指精神病] 
  (historical). another term for black bile  • [史] 同 black bile 
◙ adjective
1. sad, gloomy, or depressed  • 忧愁的，忧郁的；沮丧的
 »she felt a little melancholy.  她有一点忧愁。
 »the dog has a melancholy expression.  这只狗表情忧郁。
  causing or expressing sadness; depressing  • 令人忧郁的；表示忧郁的；使人沮丧的
 »the study makes melancholy if instructive reading.  该研究报告读来让人增长知识，却也令人沮丧。

【派生】♦ melancholic adjective
♦ melancholically adverb

【语源】1. Middle English: from Old French melancolie, via late Latin from Greek melankholia, from melas, melan- 'black' + kholē 'bile', an excess of which was formerly believed to cause depression
