★☆☆   customs
 / ˈkʌstəmz /◙ plural noun
1. the official department that administers and collects the duties levied by a government on imported goods  • 海关
 »cocaine seizures by customs have risen this year.  今年海关查获的可卡因有所增长。
 »[as modifier] a customs officer.  海关官员。
  the place at a port, airport, or frontier where officials check incoming goods, travellers, or luggage  • （港口、机场或边境的）海关关口
 »we were through customs with a minimum of formalities.  我们以最简化的手续通过了海关。
  [一般作 customs duties] the duties levied by a government on imported goods  • 关税

【语源】1. late Middle English: originally in the singular, denoting a customary due paid to a ruler, later duty levied on goods on their way to market
