 / ˈdɔ:mə /◙ (也作 dormer window), noun
1. a window that projects vertically from a sloping roof  • 老虎窗，天窗

★☆☆   dormitory
 / ˈdɔ:mitri /◙ (pl. -ies)
1. a large bedroom for a number of people in a school or institution  • （学校或机构的）寝室
  [N. Amer.] a university or college hall of residence or hostel  • [北美] （大学或学院的）学生宿舍
  [as modifier] denoting a small town or suburb providing a residential area for those who work in a nearby city  • （在附近市内工作者的）小镇（或郊区）住宅区

【语源】1. late Middle English: from Latin dormitorium, neuter (used as a noun) of dormitorius, from dormire 'to sleep'

★☆☆   dormouse
 / ˈdɔ:maus /◙ (pl. dormice)
1. an agile mouse-like rodent with a hairy or bushy tail, found in Africa and Eurasia. Some kinds are noted for spending long periods in hibernation  • 睡鼠
2. Family Gliridae: several genera and species, including the common or hazel dormouse [Muscardinus avellanarius] and the fat dormouse  • 睡鼠科： 数属，数种，包括普通睡鼠（或榛睡鼠）（拉丁名Muscardinus avellanarius）和胖睡鼠

【语源】1. late Middle English: of unknown origin, but associated with French dormir or Latin dormire 'to sleep' and mouse
