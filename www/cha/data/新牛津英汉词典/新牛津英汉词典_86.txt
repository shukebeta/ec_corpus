★☆☆   fresco
 / ˈfreskəʊ /◙ (pl. -oes 或 -os)
1. a painting done rapidly in watercolour on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colours penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries  • 湿壁画，壁画
  [mass noun] this method of painting, used in Roman times and by the great masters of the Italian Renaissance including Giotto, Masaccio, and Michelangelo  • 湿壁画技法

★☆☆   fresh
 / freʃ /◙ adjective
1. not previously known or used; new or different  • 以前所不知道的；未用过的；新的；新近的；不同的
 »the court had heard fresh evidence.  法庭已听取了新的证据。
2. recently created or experienced and not faded or impaired  • 新创的；刚经历的；新鲜的；未凋谢的；未削弱的
 »the memory was still fresh in their minds.  他们对那事记忆犹新。
  (of food) recently made or obtained; not tinned, frozen, or otherwise preserved  • （食品）新做的；刚到的；新鲜的；非罐装的；非冷藏的
  [predic.] (of a person) full of energy and vigour  • （人）精力充沛的；精神饱满的
 »they are feeling fresh after a good night's sleep.  睡了一个晚上的好觉，他们现在感觉精力充沛。
  (of a colour or a person's complexion) bright or healthy in appearance  • （颜色）鲜艳的，鲜明的；（人的面色）气色好的，显得健康的
  (of a person) attractively youthful and inexperienced  • （人）年轻可爱但缺乏经验的
  [predic.] (fresh from/out of)(of a person) having just had (a particular experience) or come from (a particular place)  • （人）刚有过（某种经历）的；刚从（某地）来的
 »we were fresh out of art school.  我们刚从美术学校毕业。
3. (of water) not salty  • （水）淡的
  pleasantly clean, pure, and cool  • 干净的；纯洁的；凉爽的
 »a bit of fresh air does her good.  呼吸一点清新的空气对她有好处。
4. (of the wind) cool and fairly strong  • （风）凛冽的；强劲的
  [Brit. informal] (of the weather) rather cold and windy  • [英，非正式] （天气）凛冽的；多风的
5. [informal] presumptuous or impudent towards someone, especially in a sexual way  • [非正式] （尤指对异性）放肆的，大胆无礼的；专横的
 »some of the men tried to get fresh with the girls.  有几个男的想要对姑娘们不规矩。
6. [W. Indian] having an unpleasant, slightly rotten smell  • [西印度] 气味难闻的；有点臭的
 »this place was covered in water and smelled fresh like hell.  这个地方泡在水里，气味难闻极了。
◙ adverb
1. [usu. in combination] newly ; recently  • 新鲜地；最近地；刚；才
 »fresh-baked bread.  刚烤好的面包。
 »fresh-cut grass.  才割的草。

★☆☆   freshen
 / ˈfreʃən /◙ verb
1. [with obj.] make (something) newer, cleaner, or more attractive  • 使面目一新；使更干净；使更吸引人
 »it didn't take long to freshen her make-up.  她一会儿功夫就把妆补好了。
  (chiefly N. Amer.)add more liquid to (a drink); top up  • [主北美] 给（酒，饮料）添加液体；将（酒，饮料）添满；斟满
2. [no obj.] (of wind) become stronger and colder  • （风）增强；变冷
3. [no obj.] [N. Amer.] (of a cow) give birth and come into milk  • [北美] （母牛）产犊并开始泌乳

 »he went off to freshen up in the local baths.  他去当地的澡堂洗澡，以振作精神。
  (freshen something up)make something look newer or more attractive  • 使看上去面目一新（或更吸引人）
