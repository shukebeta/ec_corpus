 GRAMMAR 
When much is a quantifier, it is used mainly in questions and negative sentences. much 是限定词时，主要用于疑问句和否定句: 
Was there much mess?  是不是很乱？
I don't have much time.  我的时间不多。
In sentences which are not questions or negative sentences, phrases like 'a lot of' and 'plenty of' are used instead. 如果不是疑问句或否定句，就用 a lot of 和 plenty of:
Kurama has a lot of snow ( NOT Kurama has much snow  ). 鞍马山经常下雪。
Much can also be used after too, so, and as . much 也可用于 too, so 和 as 之后: 
We've wasted too much time.  我们浪费了太多时间。
She cried so much her head ached.  她哭得很厉害，头都疼了。
Drink as much wine as you want.  你想喝多少酒就喝多少酒。
 ► Do not use much before countable nouns. Use many or a lot of . 可数名词前不要用 much，而要用 many 或 a lot of: 
There are too many advertisements on television (NOT There are too much advertisements on television).  电视里广告太多了。
When much is an adverb, it is mainly used before comparative adjectives. much 作为副词时主要用于形容词比较级之前: 
He looks much older than 35.  他看上去远不止 35 岁。
Some people are much more fortunate than others.  有些人比其他人要幸运很多。
Much can also be used before some adjectives in questions and negative questions. much 还可用于疑问句或否定疑问句中的某些形容词之前: 
She doesn't look much different with her new hairstyle.  她换了新发型，但看起来没有多大变化。
 ► Do not use much before adjectives in sentences that are not questions or negative sentences. Use very . 如果不是疑问句或否定句，形容词之前不要用 much，而要用 very: 
Tea and coffee taste very different (NOT Tea and coffee taste much different).  茶和咖啡味道非常不一样。
Much can also be used before some past participles acting as adjectives. much 也可用于某些用作形容词的过去分词之前: 
Education is a much discussed government service.  教育是个被经常谈论的政府公共服务事业。
a much admired writer  一位深受仰慕的作家 This use is mainly found in formal and literary English. 这种用法主要见于正式和文学用语中。
