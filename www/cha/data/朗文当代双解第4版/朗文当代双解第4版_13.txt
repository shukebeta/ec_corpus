→verb (past tense became) / bɪˈkem ;  bɪˈkeɪm / (past participle become)

Don't try to be clever – it doesn't become you.  不要装聪明 — 这样不合适。

 CHOICE 
become, get, go, turn, grow, come:
   become can be followed by an adjective or noun, not a verb.  become 后面可以跟形容词或名词，但不能跟动词:
Her husband became jealous.  她丈夫心生醋意了。
We soon became friends.  我们很快就成了朋友。
get is very often used instead of become, and is more usual in spoken English. get 经常用来代替 become，在英语口语中更常用: 
I was getting hungry.  我开始饿了。
Things got worse and worse.  情况越来越糟。
   go is used to say that something changes colour.  go 用于表示某物变颜色:
The sky went pink.  天空变成了粉红色。
   to say that someone feels a change in their body.  go 还表示身体的变化:
My fingers have gone numb.  我的手指变麻木了。
   with blind and deaf .  与 blind 和 deaf 连用:
He went blind.  他失明了。
   with mad, insane, crazy etc.  go 与 mad, insane 和 crazy 等连用:
The crowd went wild.  人群疯狂了。
   turn is used especially to say that something changes colour.  turn 尤用于表示某物变颜色:
The liquid turned green.  液体变成了绿色。
His face turned pale.  他脸色变白了。
   grow can be used in fairly literary written English to say that something changes gradually.  grow 表示某物渐渐发生变化，可用于带点文学色彩的书面语中:
It grew dark as we walked.  我们走在路上，天色渐渐暗下来。
   with a to-infinitive, to say that someone gradually starts doing something.  grow 和带 to 的不定式连用，表示某人渐渐开始做某事:
We grew to love each other.  我们渐渐相爱了。
come is used with adjectives like apart, undone, and unstuck . come 与 apart，undone 和 unstuck 之类的形容词连用: 
Your shoelace has come undone.  你的鞋带松了。
A few pages came loose.  有几张书页松了。
   with true .  come 和 true 连用:
Her prediction came true.  她的预言成真了。
   with a to-infinitive to say that someone starts doing something.  come 与带 to 的不定式连用，表示某人开始做某事:
I eventually came to realize (NOT became to realize) I was wrong.  我终于意识到我错了。
