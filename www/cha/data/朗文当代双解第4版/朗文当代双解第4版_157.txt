★☆☆   statistic
   sta·tis·tic / stəˈtɪstɪk ;  stəˈtɪstɪk / 
→noun1. statistics
  a. [pl.] a set of numbers which represent facts or measurements  统计数字（资料）:
the official crime statistics   官方的犯罪统计数字
  ▪ [+ for ]
statistics for injuries at work  工伤统计数字
Statistics show that 50% of new businesses fail in their first year.  统计数据显示，50% 的新企业在第一年即倒闭。
  b. [U] the science of collecting and examining such numbers  统计学:
Statistics is a branch of mathematics.  统计学是数学的一个分支。
2. [sing.] a single number which represents a fact or measurement  数据; 统计数字中的一项:
The statistic comes from a study recently conducted by the British government.  这项统计数据是从英国政府最近进行的一项研究中得出的。
3. a statistic, [informal] if someone is just a statistic, they are just another example of someone who has died because of a particular type of accident or disease
  〔死于某种意外事故或疾病的〕数据:
We can't let these boys become just another statistic.  我们不能让这些男孩成为另一项统计数字。
—statistical adj: 
statistical evidence  以统计数字表明的证据
—statistically / -klɪ ;  -kli / adv: 
The variation is not statistically significant.  从统计学的意义上看，这一变化并不显著。
⇨ VITAL STATISTICS
