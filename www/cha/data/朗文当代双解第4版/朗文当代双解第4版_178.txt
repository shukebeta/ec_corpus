 GRAMMAR 
If you used to do something, you did it regularly or for a period of time in the past. used to do something 指过去经常做某事，或者过去某段时间里做过某事: 
She used to come here (NOT was used to come here) every week.  她过去每周都到这儿来。
I used to go to that school.  我曾在那所学校念书。
 ► Do not say 'be used to'. This has a different meaning – see entry used 1 . 不要说 be used to，这个短语意思不同。 见 used¹ 词条。 
 ► Use the infinitive after used to, not the past tense， used to 后面用动词不定式，不要用过去式: 
My dad used to grow vegetables (NOT used to grew vegetables).  我父亲以前种过蔬菜。
 ► For talking about a present habit, use usually ， 描述一个现在的习惯时，用usually: 
We usually eat (NOT use to eat) around six.  我们通常六点左右吃饭。
In questions, say did someone use to ...? 提问的时候，说 did someone use to ...？: 
Did he use to fight with his brother?  他以前常和弟弟打架吗？
In negatives, say didn't / did not use to ... 在否定句中，说 didn't / did not use to ...: 
He didn't use to smoke，  他以前不抽烟。
You can also say never used to . 也可以说 never used to: 
They never used to ask where I'd been.  他们以前从来不问我去哪里了。
In formal British English you can also say used not to . 在正式的英国英语中，还可以说 used not to: 
Buses used not to stop here.  公共汽车以前在这里不停。
