→noun1. [C] a place in a port where ships are loaded, unloaded, or repaired  码头; 船坞;
⇨ dry dock : 
A crowd was waiting at the dock to greet them.  一群人在码头等着迎接他们。
   in dock
The ship is in dock for repairs.  这艘船泊在船坞里修理。
2. the docks, [pl.] the area of a port where there are docks  码头区，港区:
James arrived at the docks expecting to see a luxury liner.  詹姆斯来到码头区，以为会看到豪华邮轮。
3. [C] [AmE] a jetty  突堤，栈桥
4. the dock, the part of a law court where the person who is charged with a crime stands  被告席:
   in the dock
Three defendants stood in the dock.  三名被告站在被告席上。
5. in the dock, [especially BrE] thought to have done something dishonest, harmful, or wrong  受怀疑:
These chemicals remain in the dock until we have more scientific evidence.  在我们获得更多的科学依据之前，这些化学物仍要受怀疑。
6. [C,U] a plant with thick green leaves that grows wild in Britain  酸模〔英国的一种野生植物〕:
a dock leaf   一片酸模叶
   dock² 
→verb1. 1 SHIPS  船舶 [I,T] if a ship docks, or if the captain docks it, it sails into a dock so that it can unload（使）〔船〕进港，（使）停靠码头:
  ▪ [+ at / in ]
We docked at Rangoon the next morning.  第二天早晨我们在仰光停靠码头。
2. dock sb's wages / pay / salary, to reduce the amount of money you pay someone as a punishment  扣某人的工资/薪酬:
The company has threatened to dock the officers' pay.  公司威胁说要扣那几名高级职员的薪水。
3. COMPUTERS 计算机, [T] to connect two computers using an electrical wire  把〔两台计算机〕连接，对接:
   dock sth to / into / with sth
Users can dock a laptop to their desktop setup.  用户可以把手提计算机与台式机系统连接。
4. SPACECRAFT 航天器, [I + with] if two spacecraft dock, they join together in space
  〔两架航天器〕对接
5. ANIMALS 动物, [T] to cut an animal's tail short  剪短〔动物尾巴〕

   doctor² 
→verb [T] 
1. to dishonestly change something in order to gain an advantage  窜改，伪造:
He had doctored his passport to pass her off as his daughter.  他窜改了护照把她说成是他的女儿。
There are concerns that some players have been doctoring the ball.  有人担心一些选手在球上做手脚。
2. to add something harmful to food or drink  在〔食物或饮料〕中添加有害物质:
Paul suspected that his drink had been doctored.  保罗怀疑他的饮料被人做了手脚。
3. to remove part of the sex organs of an animal to prevent it from having babies  阉割〔动物〕;
   SYN neuter 
You should have your cat doctored .  你应该把你家的猫阉割了。
4. to give someone medical treatment, especially when you are not a doctor
  〔尤指不是医生的人〕为…治病:
Bill doctored the horses with a strong-smelling ointment.  比尔用一种气味强烈的膏药给那些马治病。
