★☆☆   manifest
   man·i·fest¹ / ˈmænəˌfɛst ;  ˈmænəfest / 
→verb [T] [formal] 
1. to show a feeling, attitude etc  显示，表明，表露〔感情、态度等〕:
The shareholders have manifested their intention to sell the shares.  股东们表明了要出售股票的意图。
   manifest sth in / as / through sth
A dog's protective instincts are manifested in increased alertness.  狗的自我保护本能表现在它的警觉性越来越高。
2. manifest itself, to appear or to become easy to see  显现，显露:
His illness began to manifest itself at around this time.  他的病到这时开始显现出来。
   manifest² 
→adjective [formal] 
1. plain and easy to see  显而易见的，明显的;
   SYN obvious , patent : 
a manifest error of judgment  明显的判断错误
   be made / become manifest ( = be clearly shown )
   明白地显现出来
Their devotion to God is made manifest in ritual prayer.  他们对上帝的虔诚在宗教仪式的祷告中明白地显示出来。
—manifestly adv: 
a manifestly unfair system  明显不公正的制度
   manifest³ 
→noun [C] 
1. a list of passengers or goods carried on a ship, plane or train
  〔船、飞机、火车的〕旅客名单; 载货清单，发货单，舱单:
